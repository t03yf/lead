Where Most Lead Systems Break (Silently)

These failures happen daily in businesses processing hundreds of leads. Most operators never see them until revenue impact becomes obvious.

Webhook Delivery Failure

Your form provider sends a webhook. Your endpoint is down for 90 seconds during a deploy. The webhook times out and is never retried. The lead vanishes.

Impact: Permanent data loss. No recovery mechanism. No visibility into how many leads were affected.

API Rate Limit Hit

Your CRM has a rate limit. During a traffic spike, new leads exceed the limit. Writes fail silently. Leads stack up in a buffer with no monitoring.

Impact: Data loss or hours of delay. You don't discover this until someone manually checks the CRM.

Data Validation Rejection

A lead submits a form with an international phone format. Your CRM rejects it due to validation rules. No error is logged. The lead is dropped.

Impact: Qualified prospects disappear before your team sees them. You can't audit what was rejected or why.

Integration Disconnect

Your Zapier connection expires due to auth token refresh. Leads stop flowing. No alert fires. You notice three days later when a salesperson asks why leads dried up.

Impact: Complete lead flow stoppage. Unknown number of lost prospects. No historical record of the failure.

Race Condition in Routing

Two leads submit forms within 2 seconds. Your routing logic isn't queued. Both get assigned to the same rep, or neither gets assigned at all.

Impact: Duplicate work or missed follow-ups. No audit trail shows what assignment logic executed.

The Cost Isn't Just Lost Leads. It's Lost Certainty.

When you can't see your lead workflow, you can't trust your metrics. You're making decisions based on incomplete data.

You cannot audit what you cannot log

Without event logging, you have no record of what happened to each lead. When a prospect says they submitted your form but never heard back, you have no way to verify what occurred. You just apologize.

You cannot optimize what you cannot trace

Your conversion metrics are based on CRM data. But if 15% of leads never make it to the CRM, your conversion rates are fiction. You're optimizing for a reality that doesn't exist.

You cannot fix what you don't see break

A broken webhook doesn't send alerts. A failed CRM sync doesn't notify anyone. Integration errors fail silently. By the time you notice something's wrong, days of leads are gone.

You cannot forecast with unreliable inputs

Your revenue projections depend on lead volume accuracy. If your lead count is systematically undercounted due to silent failures, your entire forecast model is wrong. You're steering blind.
